2021 Weird Gloop Year in Review

Through all that has happened in 2021, many of us found comfort by logging in to RuneScape and Old School RuneScape. We at Weird Gloop are dedicated to hosting the RuneScape Wikis, the best resources for the games, and we're proud to show you what we've done — including creating our most articles ever in a year. Read on to see what's new, what's changed, and what we've achieved on the wikis in 2021.

Traffic statistics

With over 1.3 billion pageviews in 2021, we may be the second-most visited wikis in the world. (The first is Wikipedia, although we won't be catching it anytime soon — it had more than 100 billion pageviews this year). About half our views came from the United States, with the wikis being visited by 241 different countries and territories in total, including a few new ones: Antarctica, Christmas Island, and Niue.

The year started out with RuneScape's 20th anniversary events in both games drawing players back; throughout the spring and summer, various quests and expansions caused smaller bumps in views. The sharpest increase came in October, when Group Ironman Mode was released in Old School RuneScape. We've annotated some of the more notable surges in traffic on the graph below.

Technical changes

In the second half of 2021, the Weird Gloop sysadmins were hard at work on a major upgrade to the wiki servers. Launching progressively between September and November, we moved our infrastructure from OVH to Google Cloud. This gave us increased reliability, greater flexibility, and faster load times for page views that hit our servers.

Below, we've graphed the wikis' time to first byte (TTFB), which measures the time it takes for your browser to receive the first byte of page content from the server. TTFB is a good indicator of how responsive a site is.

Time to first byte (7-day rolling avg.)
Average time

The wikis had an average TTFB of 270 milliseconds over the past year. For comparison, that's 20 percent faster than Wikipedia, which averaged 340 milliseconds. When combined with improvements to our CDN, these changes cut our average page load time nearly in half! Our upgrades help make us competitive with better-funded projects in pushing the MediaWiki software to its performance limits.

Highlights and milestones

  • 1 January

    Wiki merch

    The wikis started off the year by thanking our dedicated community with custom printed shirts and masks. Later in the year, editors posed with their merch for our first ever Merch Family Photo.

  • 1 February

    Old School Wiki object pages

    Dozens of brand new editors joined together to create articles on scenery objects in the Old School RuneScape Wiki. Many of these users were editing a wiki for the first time, and contributed to more than 600 new pages the wiki was previously missing.

  • 14 March

    Real-time Grand Exchange prices

    In a collaboration with RuneLite, the Old School RuneScape Wiki created a crowdsourced guide to item prices, based on real trades in the Grand Exchange. The wiki also shares the data in an API, which is used to show prices on articles, personal projects, and in RuneLite itself.

  • 15 March

    Historical graphical updates

    In an effort to document more history of the game, the Old School RuneScape Wiki began adding images for historical graphics used between 2005-2007. Be on the lookout for historical image galleries on pages for NPCs, items, and scenery.

  • 19 May

    Family photo

    In an annual tradition, members of the wiki community joined together for a creative snapshot of player photos from 2021. This year we had 120 submissions!

  • 24 May

    Vis Wax FC collaboration

    In collaboration with the Vis Wax FC Discord server, the RuneScape Wiki is now able to predict the optimum rune combinations for the Rune Goldberg Machine.

  • 3 June

    Advanced maps on the RuneScape Wiki

    The RuneScape Wiki adds support for advanced maps. These new maps include useful images and links overlaid directly onto the map.

  • 25 July

    Old School respawn times

    RuneLite users crowdsourced data for tens of thousands of spawns of both items and NPCs. The respawn times can now be found in the infobox on the top right of every page.

  • 15 October

    Philadelphia Techfest

    In mid-October, a small group of admins gathered in Philadelphia to improve the technical side of wiki hosting. The week was spent updating PHP scripts, migrating databases, and fixing CSS in preparation for the major upcoming MediaWiki migration.

  • 20 November

    MediaWiki 1.35 migration

    MediaWiki, the software that powers the RuneScape Wikis, was ending support for their previous long-term support version in September 2021. After a busy week in Coventry, England, the wiki sysadmins boldly completed the migration to keep the wikis running on a stable, supported architecture.

  • 16 December

    Recent changes sidebar widget

    The Old School Wiki launched a new sidebar widget showing the latest editing activity on the site. Check out the most recent edits, and try editing a page to have your edit featured on the sidebar in real time!

Test your knowledge

The wikis have no shortage of interesting facts, with dedicated trivia sections on over 15,000 pages. Think you know your stuff? Give our quizzes a go and see how you well you know the game! If you want to try more than one category, you'll get the chance to pick again after you finish your current quiz.

2021 was a year full of unique trials, so we're looking forward to covering all the new game content coming in 2022. Whether it's Het's Oasis, the third Old School League, or the new Trade Octagon stock market minigame, we hope you'll check out the wikis as you play and help your fellow gamers document every rat in RuneScape.

If you want to get involved with the wiki, there's always work to do, and new projects are getting started all the time. If you haven't edited before and aren't sure where to start, join us in our Discord or tweet us, @RSWiki and @OSRS_Wiki.
